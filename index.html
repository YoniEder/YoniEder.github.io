About

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Weizmann Institute of Science, specializing in contactless physiological sensing using FMCW radar. My research bridges the gap between theoretical signal processing and practical medical applications, developing innovative radar-based solutions for healthcare monitoring.

My work focuses on advancing next-generation contactless vital signs monitoring systems through multi-person FMCW radar, from fundamental algorithm development to clinical validation in leading hospitals including Sheba, Ichilov, and Schneider Children's Medical Center.

Research Areas

Signal Processing

Advanced DSP techniques for FMCW radar, including sparse signal processing, multi-target detection, and robust statistical methods for physiological signal extraction.

AI & Deep Learning

Developing neural networks for radar signal processing, including RaLU-Net for precise human localization and AI-based vital signs monitoring algorithms.

Radar Systems

Expertise in SISO, SIMO, and MIMO FMCW radar configurations for healthcare applications, from 24 GHz to 81 GHz frequency ranges.

Clinical Translation

Translating radar research into practical medical devices through collaborations with major hospitals and clinical validation studies.
